# Active Triggers (Biophysical Failure Rules)

The Cognitive Digital Twin automatically flags disaster risks when real-time telemetry crosses specific, biologically-grounded thresholds during critical crop growth phases.

| Trigger Name | Agricultural Phase | Mathematical Condition / Threshold | Biophysical Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Submergence Flooding** | Early Vegetative (Weeks 1-5) | **Peak Precipitation > 250 mm** | Heavy, concentrated rainfall during the early growth stage risks prolonged waterlogging, drowning young paddy shoots. |
| **Drought Stress** | Reproductive Window (Weeks 3-8) | **Root Zone Wetness < 0.35** for **3+ consecutive weeks** | Prolonged lack of deep soil moisture severely stunts growth and halts tiller development before flowering. |
| **Thermal Sterility** | Flowering Phase (Weeks 7-9) | **Peak Temperature > 34 °C** | Extreme heat spikes precisely during flowering cause irreversible pollen sterility, destroying the harvest. |
| **Pest/Pathogen Risk** | Entire Season | **Relative Humidity > 85%** AND **Temp between 25-30 °C** for **2+ consecutive weeks** | Warm, highly humid conditions create the perfect breeding ground for fungal diseases (like Rice Blast) and pests. |
