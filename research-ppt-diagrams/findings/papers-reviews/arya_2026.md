### A Time-Series Hybrid Multi-Model Machine Learning Framework for Staple Crops Yield Prediction (Arya et al., 2026)

1. **Brief Description:** This research aims to improve the accuracy of staple crop yield forecasting by addressing the complex, nonlinear dynamics inherent in agricultural data.
2. **Dataset Used:** International macro-level agricultural yield datasets and historical time-series production records.
3. **Proposed Methodology:** The authors designed a Time-Series Hybrid Multi-Model Machine Learning Framework, combining traditional linear time-series forecasting (ETS) with non-linear deep learning (Artificial Neural Networks and LSTM).
4. **Key Outcomes:** The hybrid approach demonstrated superior accuracy for staple crops, specifically achieving an $R^2$ of 98.4% for Rice yield predictions.
5. **Research Gaps:** The framework operates as an uninterpretable "black box," lacking Model Interpretability to explain why a crop might fail. Furthermore, its reliance on international macro-data makes it poorly suited for district-stratified, localized interventions for smallholder farmers.
